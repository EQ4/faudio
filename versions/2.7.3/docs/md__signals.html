Signals

The most important concepts in faudio are the notion of signals and processors. Both have simple definitions:

  • A signal is a function of time, for example \( y(t)=sin(2\pi\,t) \).
  • A processor is a function from a signal to another signal.

Signals can be composed to create more complex signals.

While many signals can be described by simple formulas, other signals such as real-world audio recordings have no simple representation, and must be sampled to be handled by a computer. faudio hide this complexity by representing signals as opaque types. Thus signals are conceptually continous and have neither sample rate or duration.
